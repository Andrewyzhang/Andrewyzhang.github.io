About Me

Currently 19, I just completed my sophomore year at Tufts University studying under computer science and mathematics, with a minor in music. I was born in Boston and grew up in Newton, MA, but attended high school at Acton-Boxborough Regional High School. Growing up, my greatest passion is playing the violin/viola. I especially love orchestral and chamber classical repertoire. Some of my other hobbies include tennis, biking, traveling, skiing, ice skating, and playing video games. Academically, I've always performed best in math, and I entered computer science with very little experience. As I learn more throughout college, I hope to build more projects and expand my knowledge!

A little bit more about violin and tennis, the two activities I've kept up with for the majority of my childhood:

  • Music! As a young first grader who had no concept of free will, I was granted the chance to play a fancy wooden box with horsehair on a stick. From that faithful moment, I would call myself a violinist. While solo performances scare me stiff, the communities I found in orchestras and chamber groups foster my love for classical repertoire. Junior year of high school, I taught myself viola, which exposed me to a whole new perspective in ensemble music (and also viola jokes).

  • Tennis! Something that my entire family participates in. Alongside ice hockey and swimming, it has kept me active on my feet and has been a stress reliever throughout my academic years. My Saturday mornings have been defined by heading out to the courts and playing friendly matches with family and friends, and the community that formed around me from that shared passion is one I will always cherish.
